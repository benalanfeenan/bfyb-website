Termite Treatment — Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between chemical barriers and baiting systems? ▼
Chemical barriers create a protective zone around your property perimeter that termites cannot cross. They provide immediate protection. Baiting systems use slow-acting bait to eliminate entire termite colonies. Both are highly effective and can be combined for comprehensive protection.
How long does a chemical barrier last? ▼
Professional chemical barriers like Termidor and Biflex typically last 5-8 years. We recommend professional inspections every 12 months to ensure ongoing effectiveness and to address any breaches.
Can you treat active termite infestations? ▼
Yes. We treat active termite infestations using direct treatment methods including foaming and dusting to reach termites in walls and timber. Once the active infestation is eliminated, we install long-term barrier protection to prevent future infestations.
What is a pre-construction termite barrier? ▼
Pre-construction barriers are installed during the building stage, either physically (with treated materials) or chemically (liquid barriers in trenches). They provide long-term protection from the moment the building is constructed and are highly effective against future termite entry.
