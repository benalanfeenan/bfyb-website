Water Contamination & Health Hazards

Understanding the risks and why professional help is critical

Clean Water

From burst pipes or toilet overflow (before contact with waste). Minimal immediate health risk but requires rapid drying to prevent mould growth and structural damage.

Grey Water

From washing machines, dishwashers, sinks, and showers. Contains detergents, food waste, bacteria, and viruses. Moderate health risk—requires professional cleaning and treatment.

Black Water

From sewage, flooded toilets, or stagnant water. Highly contaminated with harmful pathogens and biohazards. DANGEROUS—do NOT touch. Professional biohazard remediation required.

WARNING: Never enter flooded areas without professional help. Electrical hazards, contamination, and structural instability pose serious risks. Call BFYB at 02 5765 0065 immediately for emergency dispatch.

What should I do first during a flood emergency?

Immediately call BFYB at 02 5765 0065 for emergency dispatch. Then:

  • Turn off electricity at the main switch if safe to do so
  • Evacuate to higher ground if water is rising or dangerous
  • Move valuables and furniture to upper levels
  • Document damage with photos for insurance
  • Stay away from contaminated water and electrical hazards

Do NOT attempt cleanup alone or use household equipment. Professional immediate action prevents mould, structural damage, and health hazards.

How long does the restoration process typically take?

Timeline varies based on damage extent and property size:

  • Emergency extraction: 24-48 hours for standing water removal
  • Structural drying: 3-7 days for complete moisture removal
  • Mould treatment: 1-3 days for remediation
  • Full restoration: Weeks to months depending on damage severity

We provide detailed timelines after assessment and keep you updated daily throughout the process. Faster drying prevents secondary damage.

Can you save my carpets after flooding?

Whether carpets can be saved depends on several factors:

  • Type and duration of water exposure
  • Whether water is clean, grey, or black water contamination
  • Carpet age and material composition
  • How quickly extraction and drying begins

If carpets are extractable, we employ professional drying and cleaning. If replacement is necessary, we provide guidance on new carpet selection and installation. Rapid action within the first 24 hours significantly improves salvage outcomes.

Is flood water dangerous? What about sewage contamination?

Yes, flood water carries significant health risks depending on source:

  • Clean water: Poses minimal health risk but requires drying to prevent mould
  • Grey water: Contains detergent, appliance waste, potential bacteria and viruses
  • Black water/sewage: Highly contaminated with harmful pathogens and biohazards

We are biohazard-certified and equipped with full PPE for any contamination level. Never touch or enter areas with flood water without professional help—the health risks are serious.

What does flood restoration typically cost?

  • Water source (clean, grey, or black water)
  • Materials affected (carpet, drywall, structural)
  • Additional restoration needed (painting, replacement)
