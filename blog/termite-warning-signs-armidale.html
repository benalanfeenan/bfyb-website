Termite Warning Signs Every Armidale Homeowner Should Know

Published: March 8, 2026 Reading time: 6 min

Termites are one of Australia's most destructive pests, and Armidale homes are particularly vulnerable. With our region's climate, timber-framed architecture, and moisture levels, termite infestations can cause devastating damage before you even realise they're there. This guide reveals the 8-10 warning signs every Armidale homeowner should know—and how to act if you spot them.

Why Armidale Homes Are Particularly at Risk

Armidale's climate and housing stock create the perfect environment for termites to thrive. Here's why:

Timber-Framed Construction

Many Armidale homes, particularly older properties, feature timber frames, wooden stumps, and extensive timber cladding. Subterranean termites—the most common species in NSW—feed on cellulose in timber. Your home's structural timber is essentially a buffet for hungry termite colonies.

Moisture from the New England Climate

The New England region experiences significant rainfall and humidity levels. This moisture creates ideal breeding conditions for termites, which require moisture to survive. Poorly maintained gutters, leaking pipes, and inadequate drainage around your foundation all attract termites to your property.

Proximity to Native Vegetation

Armidale's location near bushland means termite colonies naturally occur close to residential areas. They can travel underground through soil, reaching your home from surprising distances.

These factors combine to make termite inspection in Armidale essential for protecting your investment.

8 Critical Warning Signs of Termite Infestation

Termites are silent destroyers. By the time you notice visible signs, they may have been working for months. Learn these warning signs and act immediately if you notice them:

1. Mud Tubes on Timber or Walls

Subterranean termites build pencil-thin mud tubes along foundation walls, timber stumps, and exterior timber. These tubes protect termites from sunlight and maintain moisture as they travel between soil and timber. They typically run vertically up walls or horizontally along beams. If you see these tubes, termites are actively feeding. Break one open—if it's rebuilt within a few days, the infestation is active.

2. Hollow-Sounding or Papery Timber

Tap wooden areas of your home—skirting boards, door frames, floor joists, and stumps. Termite-damaged timber sounds hollow or papery because colonies eat wood from the inside out, leaving just a thin shell. This is advanced damage, meaning the infestation is established and significant. Damaged timber may also feel soft or crumbly when prodded.

3. Discarded Wings Near Light Sources

Termite swarms occur in spring and early summer when reproductive termites (alates) leave the colony to find new nesting sites. After mating, they discard their wings. Finding piles of translucent wings near windows, light fixtures, or doors—often looking like tiny shimmering dust—indicates a nearby colony. This is a critical warning sign that requires immediate professional inspection.

4. Frass (Termite Droppings) on Surfaces

Drywood termites leave distinctive pellet-like droppings called frass. These tiny, roughly cuboid pellets accumulate beneath infested timber or on windowsills. Frass is sawdust-coloured and looks like coarse sand or coffee grounds. Finding frass is a sure sign of active drywood termite infestation, distinct from the mud tubes left by subterranean termites. Regular inspection of attics, roof spaces, and upper floors can catch this early sign.

5. Sagging Floors, Warped Walls, or Damaged Stumps

Advanced termite damage weakens structural integrity. Floors may feel springy or uneven, walls might warp or lean, and visible cracks can appear in plaster or brickwork. Timber stumps supporting your home may show visible tunnelling or crumbling sections. This level of damage means termites have been active for extended periods and significant repair work may be needed. Structural problems require urgent professional assessment.

6. Doors and Windows That Stick or Won't Close Properly

Termite damage and moisture problems cause timber frames to warp. Doors that suddenly won't close smoothly, windows requiring excessive force to open, or frames that are visibly bent can indicate termite activity. While other issues can cause this problem, termites should be ruled out during inspection. Pay attention to any changes in how your doors and windows function—they're early warning signs of structural compromise.

7. Blistering, Peeling, or Bubbling Paint

Paint bubbles and blisters on timber when moisture is trapped beneath. Termites create moisture-rich environments in timber as they tunnel and feed, causing paint to bubble and peel. This is especially visible on exterior timber surfaces, skirting boards, and window frames. While other moisture issues can cause this, termite-related paint damage often appears alongside other warning signs and requires investigation.

8. Clicking, Tapping, or Rustling Sounds from Within Walls

Some termite species produce distinctive sounds inside timber. Soldier termites bang their heads against tunnel walls as an alarm signal, creating clicking or tapping sounds. Colonies making tunnel sounds within walls or stumps may produce audible rustling at night. Listen carefully in quiet moments near wooden walls, especially at night. Hearing these sounds strongly suggests active termite infestation.

Bonus Signs: Termite Damage Appearance

Additional indicators include visible galleries (tunnels) in timber surfaces, mud coating on internal timber, wood that darkens or becomes discoloured, and visible termite bodies or nests. You may also notice a musty, earthy smell—the scent of an active termite colony. In severe infestations, you might observe termites themselves, appearing as small, pale insects resembling ants but with straight antennae.

What To Do If You Find Termites: The Critical First Steps

Discovering termites is stressful, but prompt action protects your home. Here's what to do:

1. Don't Panic—Or Disturb the Termites

Your first instinct might be to spray the termites or break apart mud tubes. Don't do this. Disturbing termites causes the colony to abandon affected areas and relocate deeper into your home or to other parts of the structure. This makes treatment significantly more difficult and allows the infestation to spread further.

2. Call a Professional Pest Control Company Immediately

Contact BFYB for an urgent termite inspection in Armidale. We can identify the termite species, locate the colony, assess damage extent, and recommend treatment. Our professional inspectors use modern equipment to detect termites invisible to the naked eye.

3. Document Evidence (Without Touching)

Take photos of mud tubes, damaged timber, discarded wings, or other evidence. This helps the pest control professional understand the scope and location of the infestation. Document affected areas clearly but don't disturb anything.

4. Avoid DIY Treatments

Over-the-counter termite treatments are largely ineffective and can worsen the problem by pushing colonies deeper into your structure. Professional treatment requires identifying the colony's location, species, and extent of infestation—something only trained specialists can do accurately.

Look for mud tubes on timber, hollow-sounding timber, discarded wings near light sources, sawdust-like frass, sagging floors, tight-fitting doors and windows, blistering paint, and clicking or rustling sounds from within walls. If you notice any of these signs, contact a professional pest inspector immediately.
